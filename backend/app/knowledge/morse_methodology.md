#### The Kidney Filtration Test
Morse's external verification method:
1. Collect first morning urine in a clear glass
2. Let sit for 30 minutes
3. Check for "sediment" (filtered lymph particles)
4. **Clear urine = kidneys NOT filtering properly**
5. Cloudy urine with sediment = good filtration
6. Correlate with iris kidney zone findings

## Analysis Protocol (Morse Approach)

### Step 1: Overall First Impression
- General cloudiness vs. clarity (lymphatic assessment)
- Constitutional color and density
- First impression of vitality level
- Overall "terrain" assessment

### Step 2: Lymphatic System Evaluation
- Check for rosary at periphery
- Assess overall haziness
- Look for tophi deposits
- Evaluate flow indicators
- Grade lymphatic congestion: mild/moderate/severe

### Step 3: Kidney/Adrenal Complex (Critical)
- Examine 6 o'clock zone carefully in both irises
- Note any darkness, lacunae, or crypts
- Assess adrenal signs above kidney
- Check for connection to nerve rings
- Correlate with kidney filtration test

### Step 4: Nervous System Assessment
- Count nerve rings (concentric furrows)
- Note radii solaris (parasympathetic involvement)
- Correlate nervous findings with adrenal assessment
- Assess overall tension level

### Step 5: Genetic Weakness Mapping
- Document ALL lacunae locations
- Note depth and type of each lesion
- Correlate with organ/gland zones
- Compare with family history if known

### Step 6: Digestive/Bowel Assessment
- Stomach ring condition
- Bowel zone evaluation (ballooning, darkness)
- Radii solaris indicating toxic bowel
- Transit indicators

### Step 7: Endocrine System Survey
- Check all glandular zones
- Look for pattern of weakness
- Note thyroid, adrenal, reproductive findings
- Assess pituitary/pineal (12 o'clock)

### Step 8: Bilateral Comparison
- Compare corresponding zones in both irises
- Note right (physical) vs left (emotional) correlations
- Identify which side shows more weakness
